04 / 14 HYPOTHESIS — 6AM —
05 · Approach
Brand as source of truth.
§ Approach

Extend, don't
reinvent.

The Vol.02 poster already defined the visual language: motion blur, editorial typography, a single orange accent, raw community portraits. My job wasn't to invent a brand — it was to extend one into a product.

Three layers, in order: tokens → architecture → flows.

01
Design system
Tokens distilled from the posters — not invented. Route orange, Bricolage display, Inter UI, Homemade Apple for the script mark. Dark-first surfaces.
02
Information architecture
Five tabs derived from actual user behaviour — runs, events, feed, board, me. No settings screen on the bottom rail. Settings aren't a tab, they're a chore.
03
Product flows
Four end-to-end journeys anchored in real club moments: signing up, booking a run, tracking a run, engaging with the community.

11 · Key decisions
Four judgment calls.
§ Decisions

Four calls that
shaped the product.

01
Dark-first UI
The club runs at 6 AM in the dark — the product should feel like the moment. Light mode exists; dark is the default.
Mood
02
Orange as scarcity
Route orange stays reserved for primary actions and live-run indicators only. Used ≤ 2× per screen. Overuse would flatten it.
Color
03
Quicket as co-host
Embed Quicket checkout with brand-aligned styling rather than replace it. Product thinking, not product ego.
Integration
04
Community, not competition
Leaderboards sort by consistency (streaks, attendance) alongside distance — mirroring the club's actual value system.
Values

How we'd measure the concept.
§ Metrics

If this shipped,
we'd watch four numbers.

Targets derived from WhatsApp-group retention + current IG engagement.
< 60s
Onboarding time
Sign up → Home, measured on real devices. Target shaves 40s off current IG-to-Quicket path.
80%
Ticket → attendance
Of paid tickets. Current baseline sits around ~55% because people forget where the run meets.
3+
Weekly runs logged
Per active member. Tracks whether the app becomes a daily surface, not just a Saturday one.
70%
Weekly digest open
The one push notification we send — Sunday recap. If this dips below 50%, kill it.

13 · Reflection
What I learned.
§ Reflection

The hardest part
wasn't the pixels.

It was deciding what to leave out. A running app could easily become a Strava clone. Keeping it specific to this club, this city, this 6 AM ritual is what makes it a product, not a template.
